## Claim Integrity

### What is Claim Integrity?

Claim Integrity is the practice of making a healthcare claim correct before it is ever created, not catching errors after it is submitted.

A claim carries the diagnosis, the services performed, and the codes a payer uses to decide payment. Most of what determines whether a claim gets paid is set before the claim is built, in patient access, documentation, and charge capture.

Claim Integrity moves the check to that point. The claim is verified against payer rules at the moment it forms, so it leaves the building clean.

### How is Claim Integrity different from claim scrubbing?

Claim scrubbing checks a claim after it has been built. It looks for formatting errors and missing fields right before the claim goes to the clearinghouse.

Claim Integrity works earlier. It validates the documentation and coding logic before the claim exists, which is where most denials are actually created.

Scrubbing catches typos. Claim Integrity prevents the claim from being wrong in the first place.

### Why do most claim denials happen before billing ever sees the claim?

A denial is a payer's refusal to pay a submitted claim. By the time billing receives a claim, the work that caused a denial has usually already happened.

The cause sits upstream, in the steps before a claim is built: patient access, clinical documentation, and charge capture. It is a missing prior authorization, a documentation gap, a charge that was never recorded, or a code that does not match payer-specific rules.

Billing inherits these problems. It does not create them. Fixing denials at the billing stage means reworking claims that were broken long before they arrived.

### What is the difference between pre-claim and post-claim revenue cycle work?

Revenue cycle management, known as RCM, is the full process of getting a healthcare service paid, from scheduling to final payment.

Post-claim work happens after a claim is submitted. It includes denial appeals, rework, and follow-up. It is reactive and expensive.

Pre-claim work happens before submission. It catches problems while they are still cheap to fix. Grelin operates at the pre-claim stage.

## The Grelin Platform

### What is Grelin Health?

Grelin Health is a healthcare software company. It builds the intelligence layer for Claim Integrity.

Grelin sits above clearinghouse infrastructure and checks claims against payer rules before they are submitted. The result is fewer denials, less rework, and faster payment.

### What does the Grelin platform do?

The Grelin platform reviews a healthcare claim before it is submitted and confirms it will hold up against payer rules.

It reads the documentation and coding behind a claim, applies payer-specific logic, and flags anything that would cause a denial. The correction happens before the claim leaves the practice.

This turns billing from a cleanup function into a clean handoff.

### Is Grelin a clearinghouse?

No. A clearinghouse is the infrastructure that transmits claims from a provider to a payer. It moves claims. It does not judge whether they are correct.

Grelin is the intelligence layer above that infrastructure. It decides whether a claim is right before the clearinghouse ever sends it.

Grelin works with your clearinghouse. It does not replace it.

### How does Grelin work with our existing clearinghouse and billing system?

Grelin sits in front of the claim submission step. Your clearinghouse, electronic health record, and practice management system stay in place.

Grelin reviews the claim before it reaches the clearinghouse. Clean claims pass through. Problem claims get flagged with the specific reason and the fix.

You keep your existing systems. Grelin adds the decision layer they were missing.

### What does it mean that Grelin is the intelligence layer above the clearinghouse?

Healthcare claims move through layers. The clearinghouse is the infrastructure layer that transmits claims between providers and payers.

The intelligence layer sits above that. It decides whether a claim is correct before the infrastructure sends it.

Grelin is that intelligence layer. The clearinghouse moves the claim. Grelin makes sure the claim is right.

## RxAI and Grelin's Apps

### What is RxAI?

RxAI is Grelin's application for pharmacy and prescription claims.

Prescription claims have their own payer rules, coverage logic, and documentation requirements. RxAI applies that logic before a claim is submitted, so prescription claims get paid without rework.

### What apps does Grelin offer?

Grelin builds specialty-specific applications. Each one applies Claim Integrity to a single area of care where billing rules are dense and denials are common.

RxAI handles pharmacy and prescription claims. Wound.ai handles wound care claims. Pain.ai handles pain management claims. DME.ai handles durable medical equipment claims. EM.ai handles evaluation and management coding. Urgent.ai handles urgent care claims.

### Why does Grelin build specialty-specific apps instead of one general tool?

Payer rules are not uniform. Wound care, pain management, and durable medical equipment each have their own coding requirements, documentation standards, and denial patterns.

A general tool treats every claim the same. A specialty app knows what a clean claim looks like in that specialty.

Grelin builds apps so the logic matches the work.

### What is EM.ai?

EM.ai is Grelin's application for evaluation and management coding, known as E/M coding.

E/M codes describe the level of a patient visit. They are among the most frequently audited and most often miscoded claims in healthcare. EM.ai checks the documentation against the code level before the claim is built.

### What is Wound.ai?

Wound.ai is Grelin's application for wound care claims.

Wound care billing depends on precise documentation of wound type, size, and treatment. Small documentation gaps cause denials. Wound.ai confirms the documentation supports the claim before it is submitted.

## Results and ROI

### How does Grelin reduce claim denials?

Grelin catches the cause of a denial before the claim is created.

Most denials come from missing authorizations, documentation gaps, and coding that does not match payer rules. Grelin checks all of that at the pre-claim stage and flags the issue while it is still easy to fix.

Fewer broken claims leave the building. Fewer denials come back.

### What return can a practice expect from Grelin?

The return shows up in three places.

First, less rework. Staff stop spending hours correcting and resubmitting denied claims.

Second, faster payment. Clean claims get paid on the first submission instead of cycling through appeals.

Third, fewer write-offs. Claims that would have been abandoned get paid. The exact figures depend on specialty, claim volume, and current denial rate.

### Who uses Grelin?

Grelin is built for the people responsible for revenue performance in healthcare organizations.

That includes chief financial officers, vice presidents of revenue cycle, and operators who run management services organizations, known as MSOs.

These are the people who feel the cost of denials directly and who own the decision to fix the cause.

### Does Grelin work for management services organizations?

Yes. A management services organization, known as an MSO, runs billing and operations for multiple practices at once.

Scale is where small claim errors get expensive. A documentation gap that costs one practice a little costs an MSO that same gap multiplied across every practice it manages.

Grelin enforces consistent Claim Integrity across every practice an MSO supports.

### How long does Grelin take to implement?

Grelin connects in front of your existing claim submission process. It does not require replacing your clearinghouse, electronic health record, or practice management system.

Timing depends on your systems and claim volume. Because Grelin adds a layer rather than swapping out infrastructure, it is faster to adopt than a full system change.

## Security, AI, and Integration

### How does Grelin use artificial intelligence?

Grelin uses artificial intelligence to apply payer rules and coding logic to a claim automatically.

The AI does three specific things. It removes manual claim-by-claim review. It enforces correct coding logic before a claim is built. It prevents rework by catching problems early.

The AI works in service of one outcome. Claims that are correct before they are submitted.

### Does Grelin replace our billing team?

No. Grelin removes the lowest-value part of billing work, which is finding and fixing errors after the fact.

Your billing team stops reworking broken claims and starts handling claims that are already clean. The team gets faster and more accurate.

### Is Grelin HIPAA compliant?

Grelin is built to operate in healthcare environments that handle protected health information, known as PHI, and is designed to meet HIPAA requirements.

Security and compliance practices are reviewed as part of any deployment.

### Can Grelin integrate with our electronic health record?

Grelin works with the data your systems already produce. It reads the documentation and charge information that flows out of your electronic health record, known as an EHR, and your practice management system.

Grelin reviews that information before the claim is finalized. Your existing systems stay in place.
